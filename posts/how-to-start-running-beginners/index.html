How to Start Running: A Beginner's Guide

2026-07-08

Running is one of the most accessible forms of exercise. Starting properly helps prevent injury and builds a sustainable habit.

Get Proper Shoes

Invest in running shoes designed for your foot type. Visit a specialty running store for gait analysis. Good shoes reduce injury risk significantly.

Start Slow

Begin with a walk-run approach: alternate between walking and jogging. Run at a conversational pace where you can still talk. Gradually increase running intervals.

Build Your Schedule

Run 3 times per week with rest days between. Rest is when your body adapts and grows stronger. Include cross-training like strength or flexibility work on other days.

Breathing Technique

Breathe rhythmically, matching breath to steps. Find a pattern that feels natural. Never hold your breath while running.

Listen to Your Body

Some muscle soreness is normal at first. Sharp pain is not. Rest when injured. Recovery is part of training.

Set Small Goals

Start with a goal of running continuously for 20-30 minutes. Once achieved, focus on distance or speed. Celebrate progress along the way.
