You laced up, stepped outside, and the heat hit you like a wall. Three minutes in, you're already questioning your life choices. Summer running is *hard* — but quitting until October isn't the answer.

The secret isn't toughing it out. It's running *smarter*. Here's how to keep logging miles when the thermometer is being dramatic.

![Woman running on a hot summer day](/images/miles-s1.png)

Here are all five rules at a glance — then we'll dig into each:

![Five rules for hot-weather running](/images/miles-diagram.svg)

## Rule #1: Time It Right
This is the big one. **Run early morning or after sunset** — not high noon. A 6am run and a 1pm run in July are two completely different sports. Dawn is cooler, quieter, and honestly kind of magical. Become a morning runner for the summer; go back to your ways in the fall.

## Rule #2: Hydrate Like It's Your Job
By the time you feel thirsty, you're already behind.
- **Drink water before** you head out, not just during.
- On runs over 45 minutes, add **electrolytes** — you're sweating out more than water.
- Carry a handheld bottle or plan a route past a fountain.

## Rule #3: Dress for the Heat
Ditch the cotton. **Light-colored, loose, moisture-wicking fabric** reflects heat and moves sweat off your skin. Add a cap or visor and sunglasses, and you've built yourself a portable cooling system.

## Rule #4: Slow Down (Yes, Really)
Your body works harder in heat, so **your usual pace will feel tougher — and that's normal.** Don't fight it. Slow down, run by effort instead of pace, and drop the ego. A slower summer run still counts. It counts a lot.

![Woman jogging on a shaded morning trail](/images/miles-s2.png)

## Rule #5: Seek Shade and Softer Routes
Swap the exposed asphalt loop for a **shaded, tree-lined trail.** Shade can feel 10–15 degrees cooler, and softer ground is kinder to your legs. Bonus: it's prettier, which makes the miles fly.

## Know the Warning Signs — and Stop
This matters. If you feel **dizzy, nauseous, get chills, or stop sweating**, stop immediately, get to shade, and hydrate. That's heat exhaustion knocking, and no run is worth ignoring it. Toughness is knowing when to call it.

## Keep the Streak Alive
Summer running builds serious mental grit — if the heat doesn't beat you, fall races won't either. **Run early, hydrate hard, slow down, and respect the heat.** Do that, and you'll come out the other side of August stronger than you started.

Now set that early alarm. Tomorrow's run is waiting — while it's still cool.
